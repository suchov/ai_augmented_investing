# ADSK — AI Analyst Summary

*Source: ADSK_ProResearch.pdf · via claude CLI (opus)*

---

## Business
- **How does the company earn money?** Autodesk sells design, engineering, and construction software on a subscription basis to the Architecture, Engineering, Construction & Operations (AECO) and manufacturing sectors. Revenue is 97%+ recurring subscription. Q1 FY27 segment mix: Arch. Eng. & Const. $970M, AutoCAD & AutoCAD LT $474M, Manufacturing $367M, Media & Entertainment $86M, Other $37M. LTM revenue is $7.5B at a striking 92% gross margin. It recently expanded into asset operations/maintenance via the $3.6B MaintainX acquisition (new Autodesk Operations Solutions division).
- **Does it have a moat?** Yes — a strong one. The report emphasizes **high switching costs** and deep workflow integration around industry-standard products (AutoCAD, Revit, Fusion), making churn "structurally low." It also cites a **data advantage**: scarce, geometry-rich data from decades of design/manufacturing workflows underpinning its industrial-AI ambitions. Effectively a **brand/industry-standard** moat plus embedded workflow lock-in.
- **Main competitors?** Bentley Systems, Nemetschek, Procore, PTC and Gen (peers in benchmarking), plus emerging AI-native niche players.

## Financials
- **Growth rates (from report):**
  - Revenue: 5-year Revenue CAGR **13.7%**; LTM revenue growth **18.3%**. Forward revenue CAGR (5y) forecast **10.7%**. Revenue path: $5.0B (2023) → $5.5B → $6.1B → $7.2B (FY26) → $7.5B LTM.
  - EPS: Diluted EPS $3.78 (2023) → $6.85 LTM. Analyst EPS forecast CAGR: FY27 $12.61 (+20.9%), FY28 $14.22 (+13.1%), FY29 $16.18 (+15.8%). (Note: non-GAAP figures run much higher than GAAP.)
  - Net income: 5-year Net Income CAGR **-1.43%** (weak historically, distorted); forecast Net Income CAGR (5y) **+31.2%**. LTM net income growth +44.6%.
  - FCF CAGR: **Not stated explicitly.** LTM FCF ~$2.4–2.8B; FY27 guided FCF $2.725–$2.8B. FCF yield ~6.09%.
- **ROIC / returns on capital:** ROE **50.4%**, ROI **29.8%**, ROA **13.0%** — strong returns, well above peers PTC and Gen. (True ROIC not separately given; ROE is flattered by a thin equity base — total equity only $3.2B.)
- **Debt / balance-sheet health:** Total debt ~$2.7B against ~$3.2B equity. Report flags **"short-term obligations exceed liquid assets"** (current liabilities $5.24B vs. current assets $4.37B LTM) and a **"moderate level of debt,"** now including ~$2B commercial paper raised to fund MaintainX. Piotroski Score is a perfect 9. Overall: highly cash-generative but not fortress-strong on near-term liquidity; financial flexibility is reduced post-acquisition.

## Risks
- **Regulatory exposure:** Data-privacy scrutiny, government export controls, and geopolitical instability could raise compliance costs and impair international competitiveness, particularly in EMEA where labor laws also complicate the go-to-market restructuring. MaintainX close required regulatory approval.
- **AI / technological disruption:** Double-edged. Bull thesis is that Autodesk's hybrid "probabilistic generation + deterministic engineering validation" plus its proprietary geometry data gives a defensible AI edge. Bear risk: more nimble AI-native competitors could out-innovate if Autodesk's validation-heavy approach scales too slowly, or if it delays practical AI features.
- **Customer/supplier concentration:** No specific customer concentration flagged. Broader exposure is **cyclical** — revenue tied to construction and manufacturing spending, vulnerable to macro slowdowns, rate pressure, and public-budget cuts affecting seat expansions and renewals.
- **Other red flags:**
  - **MaintainX operates at a loss** — margin dilution during integration, paid at ~18x forward revenue ($3.6B); stock fell ~5% on the news.
  - **7% workforce reduction** concentrated in customer-facing roles amid a go-to-market reorganization → near-term new-subscription weakness (the weakest part of Q1) and customer-service degradation risk.
  - **RPO growth slowed to 9%** and **billings growth expected to decelerate to high single digits** for the rest of FY27; the new transaction-model tailwind fades from ~3.5 pts to ~1.5 pts.
  - Stock is down ~35% over the past year; no dividend.

## Valuation
- **Report's fair value:** $295.52, implying **+39.1% upside** from ~$212. Analyst median price target $318.53 (range $235–$456), implying even more (~50%). Forward P/E ~16.5x FY27 / ~14.6x FY28; PEG 0.64.
- **Cheap, fair, or rich?** On forward earnings and growth the report frames it as **cheap** — a PEG of 0.64, forward P/E ~16x against ~20%+ near-term EPS growth, a 6% FCF yield, and a Piotroski 9. But note the trailing P/E is still 30.7 and EV/EBITDA 20.7, and Pro Tips simultaneously flag "high EBITDA multiple" and "high Price/Book (14.1)." So it is cheap on *forward* metrics but not statistically cheap on *trailing* ones — the discount depends on the forecast growth materializing.
- **What the market may be missing (bull):** The $40B operations TAM opened by MaintainX; the proven construction-M&A playbook ($1.8B spend → ~$600M revenue growing 20%+); a differentiated industrial-AI position; 92% gross margins, 97% recurring revenue, aggressive buybacks (~$448M in Q1), and 21 up vs. 3 down EPS revisions.
- **What could go wrong (bear):** MaintainX premium destroys value or the loss-making asset drags margins while integration + workforce cuts + GTM reorg all happen at once; decelerating billings/RPO and weak new subscriptions signal the "growth" underpinning the low PEG may soften; macro construction/manufacturing downturn; normalizing U.S. cash taxes in FY28 pressuring FCF.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper. This is a genuinely high-quality, wide-moat, cash-gushing software franchise (92% gross margins, 97% recurring, ROE 50%, Piotroski 9) trading down ~35% and priced at a forward P/E in the mid-teens with a PEG below 1 — the kind of "great business temporarily out of favor" setup value investors look for. The catch is that the cheapness rests almost entirely on forward growth estimates, while the report shows three simultaneous sources of near-term fragility: decelerating billings/RPO, weak new-subscription growth from a self-inflicted go-to-market reorganization, and a large, loss-making, richly-priced acquisition. **The single most important thing to verify next is the durability of underlying organic growth** — specifically whether new-subscription weakness is genuinely temporary (reorg-driven, as management claims) or a sign the core business is maturing — because the entire "undervalued vs. growth" thesis (PEG 0.64) collapses if the forecast ~20% EPS growth doesn't materialize. Secondarily, scrutinize whether MaintainX can be integrated without breaching the reaffirmed FY27/FY29 margin targets.
