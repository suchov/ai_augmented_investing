## Business

- **How does the company earn money?** Boston Scientific is a U.S. medical device manufacturer selling across cardiology, electrophysiology (EP), urology, endoscopy, and neuromodulation in 140+ countries. LTM revenue is $20.6B, split into two reported groups: **Cardiovascular ($13.3B)** and **MedSurg ($6.8B)**. Gross margin is high at ~68.9%, typical of a device razor/razor-blade model driven by implantable and single-use products (WATCHMAN LAA closure, Farapulse PFA, CRM, stone-management devices, sacral neuromodulation, interventional oncology).
- **Does it have a moat?** The report supports a moderate-to-strong moat rooted in (1) **regulatory/clinical barriers** — FDA approvals, pivotal trials (CHAMPION-AF, STAR), and NCD reimbursement pathways gate entry; (2) **switching costs / physician relationships** — the "interventional cardiology call point," physician education, and procedural training create stickiness; and (3) **brand/technology leadership** — BSX "maintains PFA market leadership globally." However, the moat is visibly under pressure: EP share is eroding to Medtronic/J&J/Abbott, and WATCHMAN faces a competing clinical narrative (CLOSURE-AF). The moat is real but not impregnable.
- **Main competitors:** Medtronic (MDT), Stryker (SYK) as peer benchmarks; and specifically in EP: **Medtronic, J&J, and Abbott**. In structural heart (TAVR), incumbents implied by the MiRus re-entry are Edwards/Medtronic (not named explicitly).

## Financials

- **Growth rates (from peer table / statements):** Revenue CAGR (5y) **15.2%**; Net Income CAGR (5y) **15.2%**. Revenue Forecast CAGR (5y) **8.67%**; Net Income Forecast CAGR (5y) **21.3%**. Historical revenue: $12.7B (2022) → $20.1B (2025), ~16% annualized. Diluted EPS: $0.45 (2022) → $1.94 (2025) → $2.39 LTM. **EPS CAGR: not cleanly stated**, but analyst forecasts project ~10-11%/yr (FY26 $3.36, FY27 $3.70, FY28 $4.09). **FCF CAGR: not covered** — the "Levered Free Cash Flow" line reads $0.0 for all annual periods (data gap), though management guides ~$4B FY26 FCF and FCF yield is cited at 5.16%.
- **ROIC / returns on capital:** No true ROIC given. Proxies: **ROE 14.8%, ROI 9.33%, ROA 8.42%**. These are decent but not spectacular for a device company — ROE trails SYK (15.2%) and ROI trails SYK (12.5%). Returns are respectable, not elite.
- **Debt & balance sheet:** Total debt **$11.0B** against total equity **$26.1B** and total assets **$44.4B** — gross leverage cited at **1.8x** as of Q1 26. "Cash flows can sufficiently cover interest payments." Debt actually declined QoQ ($11.97B → $11.03B). Balance sheet looks healthy and investment-grade in character, though the pending Penumbra acquisition and the potential $3B MiRus TAVR option (plus $2B buyback) will consume capital. No dividend.

## Risks

- **Regulatory / reimbursement exposure (material):** Reimbursement cuts for standalone WATCHMAN procedures are actively suppressing volumes; potential Medicaid cuts from the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act"; the entire WATCHMAN reacceleration thesis hinges on an FDA label change (9-12 mo) and an NCD update (~12 mo) that are not yet granted. This is the single biggest swing factor.
- **AI / technological disruption:** Not a classic software-disruption risk. The relevant "technological" threat is competitive product cycles in PFA/EP — BSX is losing share faster than expected and is racing to defend with next-gen Farapulse Ultra (H1 27) and FARAFLEX (28). AI is mentioned only as an internal efficiency lever, not a threat. The CLOSURE-AF clinical narrative is a data-driven headwind to WATCHMAN adoption.
- **Customer / supplier concentration:** Not explicitly quantified. Report notes supply-chain disruption in Endoscopy (Q1 26) and geopolitical exposure (Middle East conflict hitting CRM high-voltage business). Revenue is diversified across segments and 140+ countries, which mitigates single-customer concentration.
- **Other red flags:** (1) A dramatic **guidance cut** — FY26 organic growth slashed to 6.5%-8% from a 10%+ LRP, now called merely an "upside scenario"; (2) **21 downward EPS revisions in 90 days, zero upward**; (3) WATCHMAN standalone volumes *declining* for the first time (Feb 26), off ~30% growth in 2025; (4) Urology growing just 1% organically; (5) gross margin down 100 bps YoY on tariffs (~$200M impact) + PolarX discontinuation; (6) all technical indicators flashing Sell; (7) multiple analyst downgrades (Daiwa, Wolfe).

## Valuation

- **Report's fair value:** **$63.2** (also stated $63.23) vs. current **$45.3**, implying **~39.6% upside**. Analyst consensus median target $74.55 (range $55-$106), implying larger upside (~56.7%).
- **Cheap, fair, or rich?** On the numbers it screens **cheap**: forward P/E **~13.5x FY26 / 12.2x FY27**, a steep de-rating from FY24's 73x, PEG **0.25**, EV/EBITDA 11.5x, FCF yield ~5%. The stock is down ~56% from its high. For a device company historically compounding mid-teens with 69% gross margins, this is an unusually low multiple — *if* the earnings hold. The market is pricing in a growth-rate reset, not a collapse.
- **What the market may be missing (bull):** Penumbra (neurovascular/thrombectomy, H2 26) is excluded from guidance and could add a growth leg; MiRus/SIEGEL TAVR is optioned re-entry into structural heart with mitral/tricuspid optionality; CHAMPION-AF hit all endpoints and could reignite WATCHMAN "hypergrowth" via label expansion (concomitant procedures 25%→50% target); international is strong (APAC +12%, EP intl +30%); management is buying back $2B and reaffirmed ~$4B FCF and 50-75 bps margin expansion.
- **What could go wrong (bear):** The 10%+ LRP is widely expected to be formally cut post-Q3 26 (RBC 8.7%, consensus 8.5%, Wolfe just 7% for 2027); WATCHMAN standalone declines and EP share erosion may persist beyond guidance; the FDA/NCD catalysts could slip or disappoint; tariffs and geopolitics pressure margins. Sentiment and technicals are firmly negative — a value trap risk if growth normalizes permanently below double digits.

## Verdict

Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper. This is a high-quality, wide-portfolio franchise (69% gross margins, healthy 1.8x-levered balance sheet, mid-teens historical growth) that has been cut in half and now trades at ~13x forward earnings with a PEG of 0.25 — the kind of quality-at-a-discount setup Buffett and Lynch look for when a good business stumbles rather than breaks. But the discount exists for a real reason: the growth narrative just decelerated hard, the LRP is likely to be cut again, and the entire "return to hypergrowth" thesis leans on WATCHMAN. **The single most important thing to verify next is the durability of the WATCHMAN franchise** — specifically whether the FDA label change and CMS NCD actually materialize on the guided 9-12 month timeline and whether concomitant-procedure growth can outrun the standalone-volume declines. If WATCHMAN stabilizes and reaccelerates, ~$63 fair value looks conservative; if the reimbursement/CLOSURE-AF headwinds prove structural, the "cheap" multiple is on downward-revising earnings and the stock is a value trap. Secondarily, confirm the true FCF trajectory, since the report's FCF lines are blank/inconsistent.
