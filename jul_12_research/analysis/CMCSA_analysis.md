## Business
- **How it earns money:** Comcast is a diversified telecom/media conglomerate. LTM revenue $125.3B breaks down into Residential Connectivity ($70.4B), Media/NBCUniversal ($27.9B), Studios ($11.9B), Business Services Connectivity ($10.4B), Theme Parks ($10.3B), and Corporate/Other ($3.4B). The connectivity business (broadband + wireless + business services) is the profit engine at ~90% of EBITDA (~$32B); media/parks/studios contribute the remaining ~10% ($4B EBITDA). Brands: Xfinity, NBCUniversal, Peacock, Sky. The pending tax-free spin-off (expected ~mid-2027) will split these into a "connectivity" company and an "NBCUniversal+Sky" media company.
- **Moat:** The report implies a **cost/infrastructure advantage** anchored by an extensive hybrid fiber-coax + DOCSIS 4.0 network serving 65M+ homes/businesses, plus scale in wireless (MVNO leveraging ~90% Wi-Fi traffic offload) and convergence bundling. Some **brand/regulatory** elements in Sky's UK position. But the moat is clearly eroding: fiber overbuilds now cover ~55% of the residential footprint and fixed-wireless access (FWA) is taking share — evidenced by years of broadband subscriber losses and a 3.1% ARPU decline. Historically weak customer satisfaction raises churn/retention costs. This looks like a **narrowing, capital-intensive moat**, not a widening one.
- **Main competitors:** Named peers Charter (CHTR) and Verizon (VZ). Broader: fiber overbuilders, fixed-wireless providers (T-Mobile/Verizon FWA), satellite, and in media a saturated streaming field (implied Netflix/Disney). Sky competes in UK pay-TV/advertising (acquiring ITV channels).

## Financials
- **Growth rates (per report):** Revenue CAGR (5y) 3.62%; Net Income CAGR (5y) 13.7%. Forward-looking is far weaker: **Revenue Forecast CAGR (5y) just 0.14%** and **Net Income Forecast CAGR (5y) −5.32%**. EPS is expected to *decline* ~18.8% in FY26 (to $3.50) before modest 7%/10% growth in FY27/28. FCF CAGR: Not explicitly given, though FCF was $3.9B in Q1 26 and FCF yield is elevated (~19–24%).
- **Returns on capital:** ROE 21.5%, ROA 6.97%, ROI 7.46%. No explicit ROIC. Returns look decent but flattered by heavy leverage (ROE >> ROA/ROI). The mid-single-digit ROA/ROI on a capital-intensive network business is unremarkable.
- **Debt / balance sheet:** Total debt ~$94.6B (LTM), down from ~$105B; total equity $88.5B → **debt/equity ~1.01**. Net leverage 2.3x (management figure). **Short-term obligations exceed liquid assets (current ratio <1)** — flagged repeatedly as a liquidity concern. Management claims both post-spin entities will hold investment-grade balance sheets. Serviceable but not conservative.

## Risks
- **Regulatory:** The Sky–ITV £1.6B deal (creating ~70% of UK linear TV ad market) faces lengthy antitrust review and public-interest tests. Potential future broadband pricing regulation or structural-separation mandates flagged as a threat. Spin-off itself requires regulatory approvals.
- **AI / technological disruption:** Not framed as AI specifically, but technological substitution is central: FWA and fiber overbuilds (~55% of footprint) are structurally eroding broadband pricing power; cord-cutting continues to erode video revenue and the bundle economics that supported broadband retention.
- **Customer/supplier concentration:** Not covered as a concentration risk. (Relevant items instead: NBA contract costs driving 9% EBITDA decline; NBCUniversal reliance on major events like Olympics/Super Bowl for revenue timing.)
- **Other red flags:** 15 of 23 analysts cut EPS estimates in 90 days; net income expected to fall in FY26; broadband ARPU down 3.1% with more pressure guided; Peacock still losing money ($432M quarterly EBITDA loss); buyback program temporarily suspended for the spin; spin-off execution risk (new leadership at both entities). Note Brian Roberts **retains voting control via super-voting shares at both entities** — a governance concern for minority owners.

## Valuation
- **Fair value / upside:** Report's fair value **$37.38, implying +57.2% upside** from $23.4. Median analyst target $32.10 (+~37%); range $21–$44. Deutsche Bank Buy at $32 (SOTP methodology). Recent ratings are mixed-to-cautious (several Holds, a Wells Fargo Sell at $28).
- **Cheap, fair, or rich:** On the multiples, screamingly **cheap** — P/E ~4.5x, EV/EBITDA ~4.6x, P/FCF ~5.7x on 2027 estimates, P/B 0.97, 5.65% yield with a 6-year growth streak and 19 years of payments. But cheapness reflects a genuine value-trap risk: declining forward earnings, ARPU erosion, and competitive share loss. This is a classic "statistically cheap, fundamentally challenged" setup — cheap for reasons, not free.
- **Bull (what market may be missing):** Spin-off could re-rate the pure-play connectivity business to a higher multiple; SOTP > current price. Wireless is a real growth lever — record 435K net adds, only 16% penetration, ~$85 convergence ARPA (~half of telco peers) with free-line-to-paid conversion coming in H2 26. Broadband losses narrowed 117K YoY (first improvement since Q4 20). Theme Parks +33% EBITDA (Epic Universe). Peacock nearing profitability. Strong FCF and capital returns ($11B TTM).
- **Bear (what could go wrong):** Broadband is the ~90%-EBITDA core and it's under structural pressure — ARPU falling, ~55% fiber overbuild, aggressive FWA. Half of Q1's broadband improvement was a one-time "Legendary February" promo, not durable. FY26 net income declining; adjusted EBITDA −9%. Spin-off execution/regulatory risk; buyback paused. Peacock profitability unproven once event tailwinds fade. Liquidity (current ratio <1) and leverage limit flexibility.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper, because the price embeds so much pessimism (4.5x P/E, ~5.7x P/FCF, sub-book, 5.65% yield) that even modest stabilization of the core plus a clean spin-off could deliver strong returns; the SOTP thesis and the wireless/convergence optionality are real. But the caution is equally real: this is a low-growth, capital-intensive, structurally pressured broadband business with declining forward earnings, so it must be underwritten as a value/turnaround, not a compounder. **The single most important thing to verify next is the durability of broadband — specifically, whether subscriber and ARPU trends stabilize once the one-time "Legendary February" promo benefit rolls off (management admits >half of Q1's improvement was promotional).** If organic broadband net adds and ARPU keep deteriorating, the ~90%-of-EBITDA core is impaired and the cheap multiple is a value trap regardless of the spin-off.
