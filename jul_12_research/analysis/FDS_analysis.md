# FDS — AI Analyst Summary

*Source: FDS_ProResearch.pdf · via claude CLI (opus)*

## Business
- **How does the company earn money?** FactSet sells integrated financial data, analytics, and workflow solutions to the global investment community — asset managers, investment banks, wealth advisors, dealmakers — across 80+ countries and 9,000+ clients (241,000+ users). Revenue is subscription-based, tracked via Annual Subscription Value (ASV of $2.48B, +7.1% organic). Roughly 75% of ASV is tied to terminal-linked products; ~51% of revenue from terminals and ~25% from aggregated, non-proprietary data. By geography, LTM revenue splits Americas $1.6B, EMEA $598M, Asia Pacific $249M.
- **Does it have a moat?** Yes — primarily **switching costs**. ASV retention above 95%, average client relationship exceeding 16 years, and nine of the top 10 clients by ASV have been on FactSet for over two decades. It serves 95 of the top 100 asset managers and 85%+ of the top 50 global investment banks — deep embedding in institutional workflows. There is also a **data/scale** element (proprietary aggregated data). The report explicitly flags the *durability* of this moat as the central debate: ~75% of ASV faces AI disintermediation and data-unbundling risk.
- **Main competitors:** Bloomberg, S&P Global, Refinitiv, plus emerging fintech/AI-native data players. Peer benchmarks in the report use Morningstar (MORN) and Autodesk (ADSK).

## Financials
- **Growth rates (from report):** Revenue CAGR (5y) 9.22%; Net Income CAGR (5y) 9.87%. Forward outlook decelerates — Revenue Forecast CAGR (5y) 5.54%; Net Income Forecast CAGR (5y) 7.32%. Analyst EPS growth: FY26 +4.7%, FY27 +10.6%, FY28 +11.1%. FCF CAGR not stated, but FCF grew 11% YoY to $254M in Q3. Historical revenue: $1,843M (2022) → $2,438M (LTM); Diluted EPS $10.2 (2022) → $15.2 LTM (note LTM EPS dipped from FY25's $15.6 on margin pressure).
- **ROIC / returns on capital:** ROE 27.0%, ROI 17.2%, ROA 13.3% — solid returns, though below peer MORN (ROE 30.7%). No explicit ROIC figure given.
- **Debt / balance-sheet health:** Conservative. Total Debt ~$1.57B against Total Equity $2.03B; gross leverage ~1.5x, net debt leverage 1.2x per the new CFO. **Red flag:** current liabilities jumped to $1,064M in Q3 2026 (from ~$521M) versus current assets of only $727.5M — "short-term obligations exceed liquid assets" is flagged repeatedly. Interest coverage is strong. FactSet raised its dividend 27 consecutive years; returned $625M+ YTD FY26 to shareholders (~2x prior year), repurchasing 926K shares for $203M in Q3.

## Risks
- **Regulatory exposure:** Not covered in report (no material regulatory risk raised).
- **AI / technological disruption:** The headline risk. Rothschild Redburn's Sell thesis warns AI disintermediation and data unbundling could structurally erode pricing power across the ~75% of ASV tied to terminals. This is dual-edged: FactSet is also monetizing AI (10%+ of ASV growth from AI SKUs, Google Cloud/Gemini partnership, MCP volumes 13x QoQ), but AI "lowers barriers to data aggregation," inviting new entrants. Token/infrastructure costs are a structurally new, rapidly scaling expense with uncertain unit economics.
- **Customer / supplier concentration:** Client base is well diversified across geographies and types. The real concentration is **product**: ~75% of ASV in terminal-linked products facing structural pressure.
- **Other red flags:** Margin compression — adjusted operating margin fell ~300bps to 34% (from ~36.8%), down sequentially from 35%, with Q4 facing "the toughest comparison quarter in company history." Stock down ~41% over the past year. Elevated Price/Book (4.52x). Management declined to give specific quarterly margin guidance, hampering modeling.

## Valuation
- **Fair-value estimate:** Report's Fair Value $362.2 vs. price $258.3 → **+40.2% upside**. Note this diverges sharply from analyst consensus mean target ~$254 (implying ~-5% from current price; range $210–$340). Analyst ratings are broadly Hold/Sell with several Sells (Barclays $216, Wells Fargo $210, Wolfe $240, Rothschild $215).
- **Cheap, fair, or rich?** On a historical basis, clearly cheaper than it was — Forward P/E has compressed to 13.7x (FY26) and 12.9x (FY27), versus P/E of 32.8x in 2023 and 26.5x in 2025. FCF yield 7.71%, EV/EBITDA 11.2x. For a high-retention, 27-year dividend-grower, ~14x forward earnings looks reasonably attractive **if** the moat holds. The PEG of 1.70 and Pro Tips ("high P/E relative to near-term earnings growth," "high Price/Book") temper that — the multiple is low mainly because growth has decelerated and terminal risk is real.
- **What the market may be missing (bull):** AI monetization is inflecting (from ~zero to 10%+ of ASV growth in a year; AI-using clients grow ASV 50% faster QoQ; contract terms extended ~30% with no price compression; a top-10 bank doubled data subscriptions). If FactSet becomes AI infrastructure rather than a disintermediation victim, current ~14x forward earnings is cheap.
- **What could go wrong (bear):** The Rothschild thesis plays out — AI unbundles the terminal, pricing power erodes across 75% of ASV, growth stays mid-single-digit while token costs keep compressing margins. The 40% fair-value upside would then prove illusory, and consensus (~$254) is arguably the more sober anchor.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper. FactSet is a genuinely high-quality franchise (95%+ retention, 16+ year relationships, 27-year dividend growth, conservative balance sheet, 7%+ FCF yield) now trading at a sharply de-rated ~14x forward earnings after a ~41% drawdown, precisely because the market fears AI will hollow out its terminal moat. That is exactly the kind of "great business facing a real-but-debatable threat" setup where value can be found — but only if the moat survives. **The single most important thing to verify next: whether AI is net-accretive or net-destructive to terminal ASV.** Concretely, track whether the ~75% of ASV tied to terminals holds pricing and retention as AI/MCP adoption scales (early signs — extended contract terms with no price compression, 90%+ of MCP deals improving contract value — are encouraging but must be confirmed over several more quarters against Rothschild's disintermediation thesis). Secondarily, confirm the Q3 spike in current liabilities to $1.06B is benign (e.g., near-term debt maturity) and watch whether margins recover as promised productivity gains scale.
