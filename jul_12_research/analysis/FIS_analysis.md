# FIS — AI Analyst Summary

*Source: FIS_ProResearch.pdf · via claude CLI (opus)*

---

## Business
- **How it earns money:** FIS is a B2B financial-technology vendor selling core banking, capital-markets, and payment-processing software/services to financial institutions. LTM revenue of $11.4B splits into Banking Solutions (~$7.9B) and Capital Markets Solutions (~$3.3B). The model is ~80% recurring, underpinned by multi-year contracts on mission-critical core systems.
- **Moat:** Yes — the report makes a credible case for switching costs plus a scale/data/regulatory advantage. It serves nearly all top-50 global banks, processes 73B annual payment transactions across ~1.1B accounts, and its core systems are "costly to replace" with high retention. The Anthropic partnership explicitly relies on FIS's regulated infrastructure that a raw LLM provider cannot replicate. This is a genuine, if not impregnable, moat.
- **Competitors:** Fiserv, Jack Henry, cloud-native fintech entrants (e.g. Pismo referenced), plus Nasdaq (NDAQ) and Cognizant (CTSH) as report peer benchmarks. Longer-term threat: big-tech disintermediation of financial infrastructure.

## Financials
- **Revenue CAGR:** 5-year historical CAGR is **negative at -3.18%** (a red flag — reflects prior divestitures like Worldpay). LTM revenue grew 12.3%, but *pro forma* organic growth is only ~6.5%. Forecast 5-year revenue CAGR is 9.15%.
- **EPS CAGR:** Not cleanly given as a multi-year figure. Analyst EPS growth is ~9% per year forward (2026 $6.31 → 2028 $7.32). GAAP EPS is extremely noisy — huge prior losses (-$27.7 in 2022, -$11.3 in 2023) from goodwill writedowns, so historical EPS CAGR is not meaningful.
- **FCF CAGR:** Not stated as a CAGR. FCF target rises from $2.1B guided in 2026 to >$3B by 2028; Q1 2026 FCF surged 111% YoY to $474M. FCF yield ~12%.
- **ROIC / returns on capital:** No true ROIC given. Report shows ROE 17.2%, ROI 5.18%, ROA 7.01%. The low ROI relative to ROE hints returns are being levered up by debt — worth scrutiny.
- **Balance sheet:** **Weak.** Total debt $21.1B (jumped from $13.3B — TSYS/Global Payments Issuer Solutions acquisition), leverage 3.6x adjusted EBITDA vs. a 2.8x target. Current ratio of 0.59 means short-term obligations exceed liquid assets. Equity $16.0B, book/share $31.1. Deleveraging is the central management priority before capital returns resume.

## Risks
- **Regulatory:** High and structural — accelerating multi-jurisdiction compliance costs; the flip side is that regulation is also part of its moat. Digital-asset ventures (Project Keystone, Lyriq) add new regulatory surface.
- **AI / tech disruption:** Two-sided. FIS is positioning AI as upside (owns agent IP, Anthropic paid like a cloud vendor), but the report itself flags disintermediation risk from cloud-native fintechs and big tech. Management's reassurance on this is worth independent verification, not acceptance at face value.
- **Customer/supplier concentration:** Not quantified, but serving "nearly all top 50 global banks" implies concentration among large-bank clients; the report notes >35% of issuing contracts renewed through 2029 as mitigation. No supplier concentration disclosed.
- **Other red flags:** 16 downward EPS revisions in 90 days; FY26 revenue guidance ($13.81B midpoint) missed consensus ($14.44B) by ~$630M, triggering the sell-off; Capital Markets recurring revenue hit ~130bps by macro-driven lending softness expected to persist all year; stock down ~47% over one year. The gap between GAAP net income and adjusted figures deserves owner scrutiny.

## Valuation
- **Fair value:** Report's fair value $68.59 vs. price ~$41.80 → **64.1% implied upside.** Analyst median target $58.45 (~36–40% upside), range $37–$85.
- **Cheap/fair/rich:** Screens statistically **cheap** — forward P/E 6.62, EV/EBITDA 7.17, P/B 1.35, FCF yield ~12%, 4.21% dividend yield with 24 consecutive years of payments. The PEG of 0.03 is an artifact of distorted trailing earnings and should be ignored.
- **What the market may be missing (bull):** Recurring ACV up 24% enterprise-wide (Capital Markets +45%, Banking +13%) is a genuine forward indicator; Anthropic AI agent (targeting a $35–40B financial-crimes market) and TSYS synergies are 2027–28 catalysts not in current guidance; a clear deleveraging path to 2.8x that unlocks buybacks.
- **What could go wrong (bear):** Leverage limits flexibility and amplifies rate sensitivity; guidance credibility is damaged after the miss; Capital Markets cyclicality persists; negative 5-year revenue CAGR shows this has been a value-destroyer, and the AI/synergy upside is promissory, not yet earning revenue.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper. FIS is a moaty, cash-generative infrastructure business with sticky top-50-bank relationships trading at ~6.6x forward earnings and ~12% FCF yield after a 47% drawdown, with a plausible 2027–28 re-rating story. But the setup is a classic "cheap for a reason": a levered balance sheet (3.6x, 0.59 current ratio), a guidance miss, a *negative* 5-year revenue CAGR, and heavy reliance on adjusted rather than GAAP numbers. The single most important thing to verify next is the **quality and durability of free cash flow and the realism of the deleveraging path to 2.8x** — specifically, whether the >$3B 2028 FCF target holds without further asset sales, since that is what determines both debt safety and the resumption of capital returns that the entire bull case hinges on.
