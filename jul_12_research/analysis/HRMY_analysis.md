## Business
- **How does it earn money?** Harmony Biosciences is a US commercial-stage pharma company selling therapies for rare neurological diseases. Essentially all revenue comes from one drug — **WAKIX (pitolisant)** for narcolepsy — with LTM net product revenue of $899.1M and Q1 2026 revenue of $215.4M (+17% YoY). It's profitable and self-funding.
- **Moat?** A narrow but real one, rooted in **regulatory/IP exclusivity plus product differentiation**. WAKIX is the *only non-scheduled* narcolepsy treatment (no DEA controlled-substance burden), with >80% payer coverage, 7+ years of prescriber familiarity, and high patient persistency. IP protects exclusivity to ~2030, with lifecycle patents (Pitolisant GR utility patents) potentially extending the franchise to 2044. This is a patent-cliff moat, not a durable structural one — it erodes with the clock and is under active litigation.
- **Competitors:** Peers cited are **Neurocrine (NBIX)** and **Alkermes (ALKS)**. The report also flags an **increasingly crowded orexin-2 agonist space** with multiple late-stage competitors (Lilly's acquisition of Centessa validates the class) that could pressure the pipeline drug BP-205.

## Financials
- **Growth:** Revenue **5-yr CAGR of 40.3%** (vs NBIX 22.3%, ALKS 7.28%) — very strong; forward revenue CAGR (5y) is a more modest 11.0%. **EPS/net income:** net income 5-yr CAGR only **3.28%**, and net income growth was **-4.65%** — earnings have lagged revenue badly. Analyst EPS estimates: $4.21 (2026, +24.9%), $5.56 (2027, +33.8%), then a projected -8.8% in 2028. **FCF CAGR:** Not explicitly given; FCF yield ran 8.94%→11.2%→13.8% (2023–2025), and the report oddly reports "Levered Free Cash Flow 0.0" in most periods (data quirk), with Q1 2026 LFCF of $27.8M.
- **ROIC/returns:** No ROIC figure. Reported **ROE 17.9%, ROI 15.2%, ROA 12.5%** — respectable and ahead of both peers on ROA. Gross margin 76.5%.
- **Balance sheet:** Strong. **$878.5M cash vs. ~$160–165M debt**, more cash than debt, total equity $910.3M, book/share $15.7. Interest well covered. "Excellent" financial health score (3.81/9/10 profitability). Note: cash fell in Q1 2026 (investing outflow -$183.6M) — appears to reflect movement into investments plus licensing spend, not distress.

## Risks
- **Regulatory/IP:** The dominant risk. Ongoing **ANDA patent litigation with AET/Sandoz** on the '920 amorphous patent. Harmony settled with 6 of 7 filers (LOE held to March 2030), but an adverse ruling could theoretically allow **generic pitolisant entry as early as Q3 2026** — catastrophic given single-product concentration. The new lawsuit could take 24–30 months plus appeals.
- **AI/tech disruption:** Not directly applicable, but the analog is **therapeutic disruption** — the orexin-2 agonist class is crowding fast and could erode BP-205's opportunity by launch.
- **Concentration:** Extreme **single-product concentration in WAKIX** — the central structural weakness. No customer/supplier concentration detail given, but the entire thesis rides on one molecule until pipeline matures (2028–2032 revenue gap explicitly acknowledged).
- **Other red flags:** Q1 2026 was a **~27.6% EPS miss / ~5.7% revenue miss** (stock fell ~10.8%); operating expenses **+38.4% YoY**; COGS rising to 20.7% of sales (from 17.3%) on Novitium/Prejex royalties, pressuring margins. **Two Form 144 insider-sale filings** in early June 2026. **PEG of -2.81** reflects negative earnings-growth expectations in some models. RSI shows overbought. BD "urgency" with no disclosed deal size/timing = capital-deployment uncertainty.

## Valuation
- **Fair value:** InvestingPro fair value **$54.30 (+41.9% upside)**; H.C. Wainwright Buy, $55 target; analyst avg target $44.91 (range $28–$85). Current price ~$38.3.
- **Cheap/fair/rich:** Screens **cheap on multiples** — forward P/E ~9.1x (2026) compressing to ~6.9x (2027), EV/EBITDA ~5.4x forward, 15.4% FCF yield, P/B 2.43x — all well below peers NBIX (P/E 26.7) and ALKS (60.3). But the low multiple is the market pricing in **patent-cliff/generic risk**, not a free lunch. It's statistically cheap *if* WAKIX exclusivity holds to 2030 and the franchise extends.
- **Bull (what market may miss):** Durable underlying demand (only ~8,600 of ~80,000 diagnosed narcolepsy patients penetrated), guidance reiterated at $1.0–1.04B, lifecycle extensions (Pitolisant GR/HD) potentially pushing exclusivity into the 2040s, ~$870M dry powder for BD, and BP-205 optionality.
- **Bear:** A single adverse ANDA ruling collapses the thesis; margins compressing on royalties; earnings quality weak (net income shrinking while revenue grows); pipeline diversification years away; insider selling.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper, but with eyes open: this is a cheap, cash-rich, high-return single-drug pharma trading at ~7–9x forward earnings *because* the market is discounting binary patent risk, not because it's mispriced by neglect. The statistical cheapness is real only if WAKIX exclusivity survives. **The single most important thing to verify next is the AET/Sandoz ANDA litigation** — the strength of the '920 and related patents and the realistic probability/timing of generic pitolisant entry before March 2030. Everything else (penetration runway, pipeline, BD optionality) is secondary; if the patent estate is sound, the discount is the opportunity, and if it isn't, the balance sheet won't save the earnings power.
