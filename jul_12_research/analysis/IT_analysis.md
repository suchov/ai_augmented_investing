# IT — AI Analyst Summary

*Source: IT_ProResearch.pdf · via claude CLI (opus)*

---

## Business
- **How it earns money:** Subscription-based research and advisory services sold to C-suite executives and their teams across 90+ countries. Over 80% of revenue is recurring subscription. Segments are GTS (IT leaders), GBS (business functions — HR, supply chain, legal, etc.), Consulting, and Conferences/Events. The "Insights" segment contributes ~90% of gross profit at a 78% contribution margin. Revenue is measured largely through Contract Value (CV).
- **Moat:** Yes — primarily **brand/reputation** (45+ years, trusted C-suite advisor), **proprietary IP** (Magic Quadrant, Hype Cycle methodologies), and **switching costs** (deep enterprise workflow integration; GTS wallet retention 97% / 99% ex-Fed, GBS 98%). The near-zero marginal cost of digital content deployment gives a scale/cost advantage.
- **Competitors:** Forrester, IDC, McKinsey, Deloitte, and Accenture. Peer benchmarks in the report show WOLT.F and HCKT (The Hackett Group).

## Financials
- **Growth rates:**
  - Revenue: 5-yr CAGR **9.65%**; but recent growth has stalled — 2025 revenue $6,497M vs 2024 $6,267M (~3.7%), and Q1'26 grew only ~2% YoY (−1% FX-neutral). **Forecast** revenue CAGR is only **2.49%** over 5 years.
  - EPS: Reported diluted EPS is volatile (2024 $16 → 2025 $9.65 → LTM $10.1). Analyst-forecast EPS CAGR ~12–15% (FY26 $13.75 → FY28 $15.83). Management targets **12%+ adjusted EPS CAGR** off a 2025 base — but a large share is buyback-driven (share count down ~10% YoY), not organic.
  - FCF: Q1'26 FCF +29% YoY to $371M; rolling four-quarter FCF ~$1.3B; FY26 guidance ≥$1.16B. No multi-year FCF CAGR stated.
- **Returns on capital:** ROI 25.1%, ROA 9.18%. ROE 94.9% is inflated by a near-zero/negative equity base and heavy buybacks — not a clean signal.
- **Balance sheet:** ~$3.0–3.4B gross debt against ~$1.7B cash (~$1.2B deployable). Leverage stated as under 2x trailing EBITDA — manageable. **Red flag:** Total Equity has collapsed to $63.4M (LTM) from $1,359M (2024), producing negative book value per share (−$0.90) and a distorted Price/Book of 148.7x — driven by aggressive buybacks eroding equity.

## Risks
- **Regulatory / government exposure:** U.S. federal segment is a ~250bps CV headwind; ~$114M federal CV at risk from DOGE-related budget cuts. Management assumes flat federal CV for FY26.
- **AI / technological disruption:** Explicitly flagged by UBS as a "meaningful headwind." Free/low-cost LLM research tools could pressure premium pricing, especially for lower-tier products. Management counters that its value is *proactive forward-looking guidance*, not reactive Q&A, making LLM disintermediation a "poor fit" — and it has launched AskGartner.
- **Customer/supplier concentration:** Not covered as a specific concentration figure. Model is diversified across ~14,000 enterprise clients. ~40% of CV is in geopolitically/trade-sensitive exposure, and the federal segment is the notable concentration risk.
- **Other red flags:** CV growth decelerated to just **1% YoY** (3.5% ex-Fed), far below historical high-single/low-double-digit norms — the core concern. New business declined (GTS −4%, GBS −2%). Consulting revenue fell to $119M from $140M. Negative book equity. Growth increasingly reliant on buybacks rather than organic CV expansion.

## Valuation
- **Fair value:** $222.03 (a later news item cites $213.41), implying **~57.7% upside** from $140.80. Median analyst target $165 (high $203, low $120). Note analyst targets are well below the InvestingPro fair value.
- **Cheap/fair/rich:** Screens **cheap** on the numbers — forward P/E 9.83x (FY26) / 9.22x (FY27) vs a historical 35x+, EV/EBITDA 6.82x, FCF yield ~13%. The question is whether ~9x is a value opportunity or an accurate repricing of a structurally slower-growth, AI-threatened business.
- **Bull (what market may miss):** CV can reaccelerate as federal headwinds lap from Q2'26; huge FCF funds a ~$1.2B buyback authorization compounding EPS; vast runway (~14,000 of ~140,000 potential enterprises); IT spend projected +13.5% to $6.31T in 2026; delayed March deals closed in April (temporary, not structural).
- **Bear (what could go wrong):** CV recovery slower/more uncertain than consensus (UBS Q2 CV +1.6% vs Street 1.9%; ex-Fed NCVI may fall below prior year); AI erodes pricing power; macro/geopolitical uncertainty elongates sales cycles (~40% of CV affected); Wells Fargo Underweight, $120 target. Forecast revenue CAGR of just 2.49% suggests the de-rating may be justified.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper. The setup is genuinely interesting: a durable, high-margin, capital-light franchise with 97–98% retention and a 13% FCF yield trading at ~9x forward earnings after a 65% drawdown, with real re-rating optionality if growth stabilizes. But the discount exists for a reason, and the thesis rests almost entirely on **whether contract value reaccelerates or whether 1% CV growth is the new normal**. The single most important thing to verify next is the **trajectory of ex-Fed CV / NCVI growth** (the Aug 4, 2026 Q2 report) — confirm the March-to-April deal recovery was real demand-shifting rather than permanent erosion, and stress-test how much of the "12%+ EPS CAGR" is organic versus buyback-manufactured against a shrinking (now negative) equity base.
