# PTC — AI Analyst Summary

*Source: PTC_ProResearch.pdf · via claude CLI (opus)*

---

## Business
- **How it earns money:** PTC sells industrial software for manufacturers — Product Lifecycle Management (Windchill), CAD (Creo, Onshape), Application Lifecycle Management (Codebeamer), and Service Lifecycle Management (Servigistics/ServiceMax). Revenue is ~95% recurring; LTM revenue split is roughly License $1.4B, Support & Cloud $1.5B, Tech Service $97M. Constant-currency ARR is $2.388B (+8.5% YoY). It serves 30,000+ enterprise customers.
- **Moat:** Yes, primarily **switching costs** — deeply embedded CAD/PLM/ALM workflows are painful to rip out, supporting retention and pricing power. The report also asserts a **data/proprietary moat**: Creo/Onshape hold unique geometric and parametric product data that general-purpose AI can't access. Recurring-revenue predictability reinforces durability.
- **Competitors:** Siemens (Teamcenter), Dassault Systèmes, and Autodesk (ADSK) — all named as having greater cloud investment and broader platforms.

## Financials
- **Growth rates:**
  - Revenue CAGR (5y): **13.4%** (report figure). Recent LTM revenue growth is inflated at 27.7% — but note this is muddied by the March '26 Kepware/ThingWorx divestiture and deferred-revenue timing; forecast revenue CAGR (5y) is only **6.64%**.
  - EPS CAGR: Not given directly; analyst-forecast EPS growth is +0.8% (FY26), +7.0% (FY27), +14.4% (FY28). Trailing diluted EPS jumped from $2.06 (2023) to $10.4 LTM, but LTM net income ($1,246M) and the $4.98 Q2 EPS are distorted by a one-time divestiture gain (Q2 net income $590.7M on $774M revenue is not operational).
  - FCF CAGR: Not stated. FCF grew 14% YoY in Q2; FY26 FCF guided to $850M ($950M "normalized" ex-~$100M non-recurring items). Net Income CAGR (5y) shown as 41.2%, again distorted.
- **Returns on capital:** ROIC not stated. Peer table shows **ROE 34.4%, ROI 18.6%, ROA 19.6%** — strong. Gross margin 84.7%; perfect Piotroski Score of 9.
- **Balance sheet:** Total debt $1,381M against equity $3,859M and cash-generative operations — described as "moderate debt." A key flag: **goodwill/intangibles are ~65% of total assets** from serial acquisitions (impairment risk). Stock-based comp consumes ~30% of net income. No dividend.

## Risks
- **Regulatory:** Not covered in report (no direct regulatory exposure discussed).
- **AI / technological disruption:** Framed mostly as a tailwind (AI-driven modernization demand), but the report concedes real threat — **cloud transition is slower than Siemens/Dassault**, and failure to accelerate cloud or monetize AI could cause valuation compression. AI monetization is essentially pre-revenue: CFO says AI revenue will be "not overly material" in FY27.
- **Customer/supplier concentration:** Not covered specifically, though 30,000+ customers implies diversification; verticals concentrated in Electronics/high-tech and Federal/Aerospace & Defense.
- **Other red flags:**
  - **Net New ARR fell ~14% YoY in Q4 FY25**; H2 FY26 net new ARR target ($127M) is only ~$7M above H2 FY25 — growth is leaning on deferred-ARR flow-through, not new business generation.
  - ServiceMax SLM churn drag for ~18 months.
  - **15 downward EPS revisions in 90 days** (1 up); J.P. Morgan downgraded to Underweight ($162) citing strategic narrowing post-divestiture.
  - Divestiture of Kepware/ThingWorx narrows TAM and removes IoT exposure.
  - Macro: lengthening sales cycles, Europe deal friction.

## Valuation
- **Fair value:** Report's fair value **$170.0**, implying **+36.5% upside** from $124.5. Analyst median target $179.25 (range $130–$230); recent constructive: Barclays $185, Baird $198; cautious: JPM $162, BNP Exane $130.
- **Cheap/fair/rich:** Screens **cheap on the surface** — trailing P/E 11.5, forward P/E 15.5, FCF yield ~6.45%, PEG 0.06, EV/EBITDA 11.6, P/B 3.73 — a sharp de-rate from P/E 47–73 in 2023–25. But the trailing P/E is flattered by the one-time divestiture gain; forward P/E ~15.5 on ~$8 EPS is the honest multiple, which is reasonable but not screaming-cheap for a business growing ARR ~8.5% and forecast revenue CAGR ~6.6%.
- **Bull (what market may miss):** Sticky ~95% recurring revenue, 84.7% gross margin, deferred ARR already contracted to accelerate FY27 growth even with flat execution, U.S. Army/Mazda/BMW wins, aggressive buybacks ($2B new authorization; ~$625M in Q2), and AI as a modernization catalyst for PTC's systems of record.
- **Bear (what could go wrong):** Organic demand is weak — new ARR barely growing; the "growth acceleration" story depends on deferred backlog and an unproven 18–24 month go-to-market transformation; slower cloud transition cedes ground to better-capitalized rivals; goodwill impairment risk; SBC dilution; AI monetization years away.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper. PTC is a high-quality, wide-moat software franchise (sticky PLM/CAD, ~95% recurring revenue, 84.7% gross margin, strong ROE/ROA, disciplined buybacks) that has de-rated sharply to a ~15x forward P/E and ~6.5% FCF yield despite beating earnings — the kind of "good business, out-of-favor price" setup value investors hunt for. The skeptic's caution is that the cheapness is partly optical (trailing metrics inflated by the divestiture gain) and the growth thesis leans heavily on deferred ARR rather than fresh demand. **The single most important thing to verify next: whether net-new ARR (ex-deferred/backlog flow-through) is genuinely re-accelerating** — i.e., is the go-to-market transformation producing real incremental new-business bookings, or is FY27 "acceleration" just contracted backlog masking stagnant organic demand? That answer determines whether this is a durable compounder on sale or a value trap facing structural share loss to Siemens and Dassault.
