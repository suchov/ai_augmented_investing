## Business
- **How it earns money:** Regeneron is a biotech that discovers, develops, and sells medicines across immunology, oncology, ophthalmology, and rare diseases. LTM revenue is $14.9B, split between **Collaboration revenue ~$7.7B** (chiefly Dupixent, developed/commercialized with Sanofi) and **Product revenue ~$6.4B** (EYLEA/EYLEA HD, Libtayo, plus other product & service ~$0.79B). Dupixent is the crown jewel at ~$20B annualized global sales (+31% cc in Q1 '26); EYLEA HD U.S. sales +52% to $468M; Libtayo +54% to $438M.
- **Moat:** Report frames the moat primarily as a **proprietary R&D/data engine** — VelociSuite/VelocImmune antibody platforms and the Regeneron Genetics Center — which it claims raise discovery success rates via genetically validated targets. There's also brand/franchise strength in specific indications (Dupixent, retina). Note this is a pipeline/innovation moat, not a durable annuity — it must keep producing winners.
- **Competitors:** Merck (Keytruda/pembrolizumab, which beat REGN's melanoma combo), Roche (Vabysmo in retina), EYLEA biosimilars (Biocon's Yesafili). Peer set cited: United Therapeutics (UTHR) and Exelixis (EXEL).

## Financials
- **Revenue CAGR:** 11.0% (5-year); forecast 5y CAGR 8.32%. Revenue grew from $12.2B (2022) to $14.9B (LTM) — decelerating (recent growth only ~5.9%).
- **EPS CAGR:** Not stated as a single figure, but analyst forecasts imply strong forward growth: EPS 2026 $50.43 → 2027 $58.92 (+16.4%) → 2028 $66.85 (+14.5%). Trailing diluted EPS has been choppy ($38.2 in '22 → $41.1 LTM).
- **FCF CAGR:** Not covered in report (levered FCF rows show $0 for most periods; only Q1 '26 shows $848M). Cash from operations rose $5.0B→$5.0B LTM, roughly flat. FCF yield ~5.3–6.0%.
- **ROIC / returns:** True ROIC not given. Report shows ROE 14.5%, ROI 10.4%, ROA 11.3% — solid but not spectacular for a franchise biotech; operating income has actually declined ($4.99B in '22 → $3.84B LTM) even as revenue rose, signaling margin/mix pressure (heavy R&D ~$6.4B).
- **Balance sheet:** Excellent. $15.8B net cash, total debt only $2.7B against $31.4B equity, book/share $306. Liquid assets exceed short-term obligations; interest well covered. New $3B buyback (~$3.4B total available). This is a fortress balance sheet.

## Risks
- **Regulatory:** Meaningful. **DOJ False Claims Act complaint** on EYLEA Medicare pricing; IRA Medicare negotiation and **Most-Favored-Nation (MFN) pricing** exposure (REGN signed an MFN agreement in Q1 '26) — could cap pricing on key drugs, notably Dupixent.
- **AI / tech disruption:** Not covered as a threat; report presents REGN's own genomics/AI-adjacent platforms as a strength, not a vulnerability.
- **Concentration:** High and flagged repeatedly — revenue heavily concentrated in **Dupixent and the EYLEA franchise**. Legacy EYLEA U.S. sales fell 36% YoY with biosimilar launches accelerating in H2 '26. Also third-party manufacturing dependency caused a Limerick, Ireland interruption that cut GAAP gross margin to 76% (vs 86% non-GAAP).
- **Other red flags:** **Binary pipeline risk** — the May '26 Phase 3 melanoma failure (fianlimab + cemiplimab, p=0.0627, missed despite 5.1-mo PFS benefit) wiped out a $2–3B opportunity and ~$9B of market cap, triggering a Citi downgrade and broad target cuts. 48-candidate pipeline = execution/resource risk. EPS revisions negative (11 up / 12 down over 90d).

## Valuation
- **Fair value / upside:** Report's fair value **$899.79, implying +33.1%** from $649.8. Analyst consensus median target $824.56 (~+28.9%). Recent post-miss targets cluster lower ($700–$860; RBC Hold $696).
- **Cheap/fair/rich:** Screens **cheap on cash flow/earnings** — forward P/E 12.9x (falling to 9.7x by '28), EV/EBITDA 9.68x, P/B 2.19x, FCF yield ~6%, beta 0.24. For a profitable, net-cash biotech with mid-teens forecast EPS growth, that's a modest multiple. Caveat: **PEG 3.61** and a Pro Tip warning it trades "at a high P/E relative to near-term earnings growth" — so cheapness leans on the *forecast* growth reaccelerating.
- **Bull (what market may be missing):** Dupixent multi-indication runway (9 indications, COPD/CSU adds), EYLEA HD replacing legacy EYLEA, Sanofi development balance repaid end-Q2 '26 → profit-share step-up starting Q3 '26, and multiple '26 catalysts (cemdisiran gMG FDA Q4, garetosmab FOP PDUFA Aug, obesity/anticoagulation programs).
- **Bear (what could go wrong):** Further pipeline misses (48 binary bets), accelerating EYLEA biosimilar erosion, MFN/IRA pricing caps hitting Dupixent, and declining operating income despite revenue growth. Fair value is model-dependent and just got cut across the Street.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient, owner-minded investor should want to dig deeper. You have a fortress balance sheet ($15.8B net cash, negligible debt), consistent profitability, aggressive buybacks, and a genuinely cheap-looking multiple (12.9x forward, 9.7x '28) on a company whose recent 12% selloff was driven by a single trial miss rather than a broken franchise. That said, the thesis is not "buy a wide-moat compounder" — it's "buy a pipeline-dependent biotech at a discount," and the concentration in two franchises plus pricing-reform overhang are real. **The single most important thing to verify next: the durability and growth trajectory of Dupixent** — specifically how much of the forward EPS reacceleration (and the $900 fair value) depends on Dupixent continuing to compound, and how exposed that specific product is to IRA/MFN pricing pressure and eventual biosimilar/lifecycle competition. If Dupixent's runway holds, the rest of the pipeline is optionality; if it doesn't, the cheap multiple is a value trap.
