# RMV — AI Analyst Summary

*Source: RMV_ProResearch.pdf · via claude CLI (opus)*

---

## Business
- **How does it earn money?** Rightmove runs an asset-light online property portal — the dominant UK marketplace connecting buyers, renters and property professionals. Revenue comes from estate/letting agents paying subscription-style fees to list and advertise (agency ARPA), plus fast-growing "strategic" lines: mortgage services (up ~50% YoY), rental services and commercial property (the three grew 25% collectively in FY25). FY25 revenue was £425.1M (+9%).
- **Moat?** Yes — a strong one. A self-reinforcing **network effect** (consumers go where the listings are; agents go where the consumers are), reinforced by **brand**: 80%+ of consumer time spent on UK property portals and 90%+ brand awareness. Combined with an asset-light model this yields a ~70% underlying operating margin and ROE of ~276% — hallmarks of a genuine barrier to entry.
- **Competitors:** AutoTrader (AUTO) and Scout24 (0QYP) are cited as classifieds peers, though not direct portal rivals. The direct threats named are **OnTheMarket (now owned by CoStar Technologies)** as a portal challenger, and AI chatbots as a potential disintermediating force.

## Financials
- **Growth rates:** Revenue CAGR (5y) **15.6%**; Net Income CAGR (5y) **14.6%**; forward Revenue CAGR (5y) ~9.1%, forward Net Income CAGR ~9.0%. Analyst EPS growth of ~6% / 10% / 13% for FY26–FY28. FCF CAGR: not explicitly given, but FCF yield rose from 4.2% (FY24) to 7.0% (FY25) — a step change. Revenue history: £332.6M (FY22) → £425.1M (FY25).
- **Returns on capital:** Exceptional but flattered by an asset-light balance sheet. Reported ROE ~266–276%, ROI 254%, ROA 176%. The report gives no explicit ROIC. These figures are so high because equity/assets are tiny (£82.3M equity, £128.2M total assets) — real economic returns are enormous but the ratios overstate the point; skepticism warranted on how they're computed.
- **Balance sheet:** Very healthy. More cash than debt (total debt just £7.2M), liquid assets exceed short-term obligations, interest cover ample, 5-year dividend growth streak, 2.37% yield. Note tiny book value (£0.11/share) drives an eye-watering Price/Book of ~40x — a valuation flag, not a solvency one.

## Risks
- **Regulatory:** A live lawsuit at the Competition Appeal Tribunal alleging estate agents were systematically overcharged. Statutory fines are capped at ~£42.5M (manageable), but **civil damages in collective proceedings are uncapped** and could structurally constrain pricing power. This is the most material named risk.
- **AI / tech disruption:** The central bear thesis behind the ~41% price fall. AI search tools/chatbots could deliver property info directly to consumers and bypass the portal. Rightmove is spending ~£12M in 2026 on AI (31 live projects, 100+ hires) partly defensively — this depresses near-term profit growth to just 3–5% in FY26 (vs 4–9% prior).
- **Customer/supplier concentration:** Not covered explicitly, but revenue is heavily concentrated in **one product category (UK agency listings) and one geography (UK)** — cyclically exposed to housing transaction volumes and agent branch closures.
- **Other red flags:** New Optimiser Edge tier already >5,000 users, which Goldman argues caps further agency ARPA upside (their agent estimates run 1–3% below consensus). Macro: UK mortgage-rate pressure tied to the Iran conflict / Strait of Hormuz energy squeeze.

## Valuation
- **Fair value:** InvestingPro estimates ~£6.52 (652p), implying **~45% upside** from £4.49. Note the report is internally inconsistent — two embedded news items quote InvestingPro FV of £6.49/£6.48 as a *33% downside* against a then-higher reference price, so treat the precise figure as soft.
- **Cheap, fair, or rich?** On its own history, the stock looks **cheap**: forward P/E has compressed from ~25x (FY24) to ~14.4x (FY26E), EV/EBITDA ~10.4x, FCF yield 7%. But the PEG of 1.01 says it's roughly *fairly* valued relative to its (now-slower) growth, and Price/Book ~40x and P/Sales ~7.9x remain optically rich. Net: reasonably valued-to-cheap for a business of this quality *if* the moat holds.
- **What the market may be missing (bull):** AI disintermediation fears may be overblown (Goldman itself thinks so); the de-rating prices in structural decline that hasn't shown up in the numbers, while strategic segments (mortgage +50%) keep compounding. **What could go wrong (bear):** slowing profit growth (3–5%), uncapped legal damages, CoStar/OnTheMarket competition, and a genuine AI-driven traffic erosion that permanently breaks the network effect. Analysts are split — 8 buy / 4 hold / 5 sell, targets 404p to 765p.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should want to dig deeper. This is a rare asset-light, ~70%-margin, net-cash monopoly trading at ~14x forward earnings and a 7% FCF yield after a 41% drawdown, with the sell-off driven by *fears* (AI, macro, litigation) rather than deteriorating results — exactly the setup where quality is mispriced. The disqualifying scenario is real, though: if AI search genuinely disintermediates the portal, the network effect unravels and no multiple is cheap enough. The single most important thing to verify next is **whether Rightmove's traffic and pricing dominance is actually holding** — i.e., current-year agent numbers, ARPA trajectory, and consumer visit share versus AI tools and CoStar/OnTheMarket — since that, not the housing cycle, determines whether the moat is intact. The July 24 earnings release is the near-term checkpoint.
