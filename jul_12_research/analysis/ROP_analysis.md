## Business
- **How it earns money:** Roper acquires and operates a portfolio of ~21+ market-leading vertical software and technology-enabled product businesses (CentralReach, Aderant, Vertafore, Deltek, DAT, ConstructConnect, Neptune, Verathon, etc.). Revenue is largely recurring — ~85% recurring in the Application Software segment — split across Application Software, Network Software, and Technology Enabled Products (TEP) segments. LTM revenue $8.1B; Q1 2026 revenue $2.1B (+11% YoY, 6% organic).
- **Moat:** Yes — primarily **high switching costs** (mission-critical, deeply embedded niche software with mid-90s gross retention). Secondary: **brand/leadership** in narrow verticals and a **cost-of-capital advantage** in M&A (new $3.5B revolver, cheap capital vs. PE-backed rivals). Notably, the report flags a *weakness*: the decentralized structure has **no cross-portfolio network effects** — this is a collection of moats, not one platform moat.
- **Competitors:** Peers benchmarked are Tyler Technologies (TYL) and Intuit (INTU). The report cites structural competition from **PE-backed software firms and new AI entrants** bidding for the same vertical assets.

## Financials
- **Growth (period stated where given):**
  - Revenue CAGR (5y): **14.5%**; forecast revenue CAGR (5y): **10.7%**.
  - Net income CAGR (5y): **10.1%**; net income growth (LTM): 14.4%.
  - FCF: trailing-12-month FCF $2.5B, compounding at a **19% CAGR over 3 years** (15% ex-Section 174). FCF-per-share grew 15% YoY in Q1 2026 (buybacks).
  - EPS: no clean multi-year CAGR given; forward analyst EPS growth ~8–10% annually through FY28 (FY26 $22.52, +9.7%).
- **Returns on capital:** ROE 9.01%, ROI 6.35%, ROA 5.20%. **These are modest** — depressed by the acquisition-heavy balance sheet loaded with goodwill/intangibles (book/share $182.8). A true ROIC is not provided, and reported returns look unimpressive for a "compounder."
- **Balance sheet:** Total debt $10.5B; **net leverage 3.1x EBITDA** (up from 2.9x at YE25) driven by aggressive buybacks. Total equity $18.8B. **Red flag: short-term obligations exceed liquid assets** (current assets $1.9B vs. current liabilities $3.6B). Cash-flow health rating only 5/10. Interest coverage not explicitly stated. Dividend: 12 consecutive annual increases, 35 years of payments, 1.0% yield.

## Risks
- **Regulatory:** Data privacy, government-contracting compliance, and cybersecurity exposure. Most concretely, **Deltek GovCon softness** from federal procurement disruption, agency reorganizations, and budget uncertainty — no recovery underwritten in guidance; delayed "One Big Beautiful Bill" benefit.
- **AI / tech disruption:** Double-edged. Bull framing = AI monetization is a TAM expansion (CentralReach AI-influenced bookings 75% of new business). Bear framing (from Threats) = **"broader AI disruption risk to vertical software incumbents"** — the same AI could erode moats of Roper's own businesses.
- **Customer/supplier concentration:** Not explicitly quantified. Cyclical end-market exposure is flagged: **DAT (freight market)** and **Neptune (water metering, bronze ingot input-cost inflation)**. Supplier cost pressure at Neptune is persistent.
- **Other red flags:** Rising leverage limiting M&A flexibility; high goodwill/intangibles → **impairment risk**; core EBITDA margin contracted 70 bps YoY in Q1 2026; 9 downward vs. 6 upward EPS revisions (90d); Q2 2026 guidance implies a sequential organic step-down; RSI overbought; and stock down ~42% over the year despite beats — the market is voting against the story.

## Valuation
- **Fair value:** InvestingPro fair value **$497.7 → +36.7% upside** vs. $364.2 price. Analyst targets range $349.78–$550, median ~$451.72 (Raymond James $500 Strong Buy, Piper Sandler $540; but Barclays Sell/$373 and two RBC Holds).
- **Cheap, fair, or rich?** On its own multiple history it looks **cheaper than it was**: P/E compressed from 47x (2023) to 21.4x LTM / 16.2x forward; EV/EBITDA from 26x to 14.4x; FCF yield up to ~7%. This is a genuine de-rating of a former premium compounder. That said, ~7% FCF yield and 16x forward earnings for ~5–6% organic growth is **fair, not screamingly cheap** — the multiple compression mostly reflects decelerating organic growth, not a bargain.
- **What the market may be missing (bull):** ~$1B maintenance base converting to SaaS at 2–2.5x revenue lift; AI monetization across 21 businesses; $5B+ capital deployment capacity into distressed vertical-software assets; heavy buybacks compounding FCF/share (~6% of shares retired since Nov 2025).
- **What could go wrong (bear):** Organic growth stuck at 5–6% with no acceleration; GovCon (Deltek), freight (DAT), and Neptune all dragging; leverage at 3.1x constraining the M&A engine that IS the business model; and further multiple compression if organic growth never inflects.

## Verdict
Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper, but with skepticism, not enthusiasm. The setup is attractive on paper: a durable, high-retention (mid-90s), recurring-revenue collection of niche software leaders that has de-rated ~42% to a ~7% FCF yield and 16x forward earnings, with management aggressively retiring shares. But the report exposes the core tension — this is an **M&A-and-leverage flywheel** running with rising net debt (3.1x), reported returns on capital that are unremarkable (ROI ~6%, ROE ~9%) once you account for goodwill, and organic growth stuck at 5–6% with segment-specific drags (Deltek, DAT, Neptune). The single most important thing to verify next is **cash return on invested capital excluding goodwill/intangibles and the true organic growth trajectory** — i.e., is Roper actually a compounder earning high returns on the businesses it owns, or a serial acquirer whose reported growth depends on continued cheap-leverage M&A that 3.1x net debt now threatens to choke off?
