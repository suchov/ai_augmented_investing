# WDAY — AI Analyst Summary

*Source: WDAY_ProResearch.pdf · via claude CLI (opus)*

## Business

- **How does the company earn money?** Workday sells enterprise cloud applications for human capital management (HCM) and financial management via subscription. Of $9.9B LTM revenue, subscription services are ~$9.1B and professional services ~$726M. It serves 11,500+ organizations, including 65%+ of the Fortune 500. Revenue is recurring, backed by a total subscription backlog of $27.29B and 12-month CRPO of $8.81B (up 15.5% YoY).
- **Does it have a moat? What kind?** Yes — primarily **switching costs**. The unified "Power of One" platform (HR, payroll, talent, finance on one codebase) creates deep integration and high switching costs, evidenced by 97% gross revenue retention. Secondary elements: **brand/scale** (dominant cloud HCM position) and an emerging **data moat** via AWS Data Cloud integration positioning it as the "enterprise AI data backbone." Gross margin of ~75.8% reflects real pricing power.
- **Main competitors:** Oracle, SAP, Microsoft, ServiceNow, UKG, and ADP. The report notes Workday's penetration in financial management lags its HCM strength — competition is heaviest where it is weakest.

## Financials

- **Revenue CAGR:** 17.2% (5-year, per peer benchmarks); LTM revenue growth 13.3%. Forward revenue forecast CAGR (5y) 10.6% — a clear deceleration. Subscription revenue guided to just 12–13% for FY27.
- **EPS CAGR:** Not given as a clean historical figure (GAAP EPS is volatile: -$1.44 FY23, $5.21 FY24, $1.95 FY25, driven by tax/one-off items). Forward diluted-EPS growth: 16.3% (FY27), 17.8% (FY28), 16.0% (FY29) on analyst estimates.
- **FCF CAGR:** Not cleanly stated. FY27 FCF guidance is $3.18B (15% growth); Q1 FY27 operating cash flow rose 52% YoY to $696M. FCF yield ~8.73%.
- **ROIC / returns on capital:** No true ROIC given. Reported ROE 10.9%, ROI 7.71%, ROA 5.09% — mediocre and notably below peers (CRM ROE 16.9%, ADBE 63.0%). This is a real weakness given the moat claim; returns are diluted by heavy R&D/SG&A and stock-based comp.
- **Debt / balance-sheet health:** Total debt $3.8B against $4.4B cash and marketable securities and $6.68B equity — described as "moderate debt." Ended Q1 with net cash. Aggressive buybacks ($1.6B in Q1). InvestingPro rates financial health "GREAT." Balance sheet is sound.

## Risks

- **Regulatory / legal:** A federal class-action (filed 2023) alleging AI bias in Workday's HR screening tools survived dismissal and proceeds under California law and the ADA — the first broad legal challenge to algorithmic hiring. Claims for Black applicants, women, and those over 40 remain. Material legal, reputational, and compliance risk that could slow AI adoption.
- **AI / technological disruption:** Double-edged. Workday is monetizing agentic AI fast (~$500M ARR, +200% new ACV YoY), but LLM providers and new entrants could disrupt seat-based enterprise software. Also flagged: customer workforce reductions / seat compression (tech-sector FTEs flat-to-declining) could erode seat-based revenue.
- **Customer / supplier concentration:** Not covered as a specific concentration figure. Related concern: 65%+ Fortune 500 penetration means the large-enterprise TAM is maturing, forcing reliance on riskier mid-market/international/adjacent-vertical expansion.
- **Other red flags:** Growth decelerating (subscription growth down to 12–13%); guidance reiterated, not raised, despite a Q1 beat, disappointing investors; Q1 partly flattered by deals slipping from Q4 ("one quarter does not make a year"); total backlog grew only 11% vs. CRPO 15.5% (duration compression); GAAP margins trail non-GAAP by ~18–19 points (heavy stock-based comp); elongated sales cycles in government-funded healthcare/education verticals. CEO Bhusri returned Feb '26 — a leadership reset signals prior execution concerns.

## Valuation

- **Fair-value estimate:** InvestingPro Fair Value $214.58 vs. price $137.90 → **55.6% implied upside**. Analyst median target $170.59 (range $115–$275), implying ~16% upside — a large gap between the model and the sell side.
- **Cheap, fair, or rich?** Signals are genuinely mixed. Trailing P/E 40.2x and EV/EBITDA 22.2x look rich; but forward P/E ~13.1x (FY27), PEG 0.54, and FCF yield ~8.7% look cheap for a company still growing revenue low-teens with 75%+ gross margins. The stock is down ~43% from its 52-week high. On forward metrics it screens **cheap-to-fair**; the market is clearly skeptical the growth/margin trajectory justifies the historical multiple.
- **What the market may be missing (bull):** AI monetization inflecting (AI-inclusive deals ~50% larger, Flex Credits, Deployment Agent targeting 30–50% lower implementation cost to unlock mid-market), high retention/backlog visibility, and back-half-weighted FY28 AI upside not yet in reported numbers.
- **What could go wrong (bear):** Growth keeps decelerating as Fortune 500 saturates, AI bias lawsuit escalates, seat compression bites, margins stay pressured by AI spend, and mid-market/international expansion under-delivers on execution — collapsing the "cheap on forward earnings" thesis if those forward estimates prove optimistic.

## Verdict

Yes — a patient value investor should dig deeper. Workday is a high-quality, sticky, cash-generative franchise (97% retention, 75%+ gross margins, net cash, aggressive buybacks) trading at a compressed forward multiple after a ~43% drawdown, with a credible AI-monetization option that isn't yet in reported revenue. But the skeptic's flags are real: decelerating growth, mediocre ROE/ROIC relative to peers, an ~18–19 point GAAP-to-non-GAAP margin gap that hints at heavy stock-based comp, and a Q1 flattered by slipped deals. The single most important thing to verify next is **the durability and quality of the forward earnings the cheap ~13x P/E rests on** — specifically, reconcile GAAP vs. non-GAAP earnings (quantify stock-based comp and its dilutive effect on the buyback-driven share count) and confirm whether AI ACV growth is genuinely reaccelerating subscription revenue in H2 FY27/FY28 or merely offsetting core large-enterprise maturation.
